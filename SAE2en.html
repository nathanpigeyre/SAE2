THE SKI






Arnold Lunn was an Englishman who found refuge in Switzerland, close to the mountains which for him was one of the most beautiful things in the world. Arnold Lunn is the inventor of modern skiing and the inventor of championships because he enjoyed organist ski races in Switzerland as a young man. Of course the Nordic people had been skiing for much longer but for them it was a necessity whereas for Arnold it was much more. He is considered to have invented, in 1922, the slalom and downhill, disciplines adopted by the International Ski Federation in the 1930s.



















The first alpine skiing competition, a kind of rudimentary downhill race, took place in Oslo around 1850. A few decades later, skiing won over the rest of Europe and the United States of America, where miners organised ski competitions for winter entertainment. The first slalom was held in Mürren (Switzerland) in 1922, and two years later the race that would become the first Olympic Alpine skiing event was held.








"With Piou Piou, I discover the snow. He moves around on skis on a flat surface and slides down the slope.
The teddy bear medal. He controls his speed and makes turns reproducing the movement of the snake. He slides with his skis parallel to the slope and stops with a snowplough turn.
The snowflake medal. He controls his speed and crosses the slope line in snowplough turns. He stays balanced by sliding faster with skis parallel, across and down the slope, on both skis or from one ski to the other, on smooth terrain on a gentle slope. Can brake and stop and start to skid.
The first star medal. He links elementary skidded turns taking into account the external elements . Can balance while sliding across slight to medium slopes. Discover skids.
The second star medal. The skier can link refined elementary turns, cross the line of the slope with skis parallel, and make turns with a rounded skid, taking into account the external elements. Crosses hollows and bumps, across or facing the slope, while remaining balanced.
The third star medal. Can link basic turns on parallel skis, with short and medium radii, taking into account and adapting to external elements. Controls balance in a schuss on varied terrain. Can improve lateral balance by using a series of side slips interspersed with direct tracks in traverses.
The bronze star medal. He refines his basic turns by reducing skidding. He can perform sculls or wide turns, taking into account and adapting to the external elements. Controls balance in schuss by taking off on rounded bumps, on the track or in a prepared area. He/she learns to ski in all types of snow.
The gold star medal. He discovers the practice of alpine skiing in all its forms. He can ski without stopping on slopes with large vertical drops, on all types of snow, taking into account the external elements and respecting the rules of conduct for skiers.

Ski touring, the "goal" is to climb to the top of a snowy mountain with the necessary equipment, most often sealskins to avoid sliding backwards and skis with the heel off. So at the top you just have to remove the sealskin and put your bindings back on and you can go down to the bottom. Of course, for this practice you need to have already done some alpine skiing and with a relatively good level (knowing how to ski down the reds without problems and having already done some off-piste skiing). Indeed, the problem encountered in ski touring is the unpredictability of the snow : it can be frozen, crusty, powdery.












Alpine skiing is special in that you will need a lift to practice it in any case. Unlike ski touring, alpine skiing runs are marked and used much more often, which reduces the danger. Alpine skiing is practiced with poles, often with a helmet for more safety, glasses because the sun in the mountains with the reflection on the snow can attack your retina and of course shoes adapted and reinforced to avoid at most injuries. The risk of injury is still very high as skiing is possible at speeds of up to 90km/h, at which a collision can be fatal.

















Cross-country skiing is also quite particular compared to ski touring and alpine skiing because it does not have the same skis, in fact, cross-country skis are very thin and very light which allows them to take more speed but they are also longer. The common point between alpine skis and cross-country skis is that the foot is attached to the ski. Cross-country skiing is practiced on fairly flat terrain and is most often practiced for championships.























Piau Engaly is the ski resort I grow up in, located in the centre of the Pyrenees, with 11 lifts and a record-breaking 2600m ski run. It is the highest ski run in the Pyrenees and has snow every year. Since I was 6 years old we have been going to this resort during the February holidays and skiing all day, I know every run as well as the bars. Piau Engaly is a very good ski resort opened since 1971, it is quite famous and quite big.





















Since I was little I have always loved skiing, being able to go very fast on the white slopes, having tingling thighs when skidding, races with my father and brother are very good memories. As far as my level of skiing is concerned I learned very quickly in 3 years I already had my gold star and I even participated in the arrow which is a tournament where I received the smallest award but it is still a victory.
